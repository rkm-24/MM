About Mandir

Thakur Raj Gopal Ji Maharaj Mandir was built in 1871 by the efforts of 'Shri Kunwar Lal ji' who was from the royal family of Madanpur. Structure of this mandir is a beautiful harmony of North & South Indian Architectural Engineering.

Thakur Raj Gopal Ji Mandir is built with the traditional principles of Vastu based on Ranganathaswami (Vishnu) Temple in Shrirangapatna, Karnataka, India.

It is the largest temples of its kind in district Firozabad (previously in Mainpuri distt.) of Uttar Pradesh, India and one of very few such temples pan India.

Special features that make it distinct are like regular seva-pooja, celebrating festivals, performing various rituals in traditional way according to vedic procedures and norms.

This attracts the people of nearby villages and towns to visit the temple and join the activities for the blessing of Raj Gopal Ji Maharaj.

The festivals and rituals are taken from the parent temple from Shri Rang Nath Ji ka Mandir, Vrindavan as the pujari jis and other staff follow all the standard procedures of daily pooja paddhati as it happens in Rangnatha ji Temple of Vrindavan.
